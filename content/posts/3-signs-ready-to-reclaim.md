## The Quiet Knowing

Most career pivots don't begin with a dramatic event. They begin with a quiet knowing — a persistent, low-grade sense that something isn't right. You show up, you perform, you deliver. But somewhere between the meetings and the milestones, you lost the thread back to yourself.

Here are the three signs that tell me someone is ready to do the real work.

## Sign 1: You've Stopped Being Curious

Early in your career, you asked questions. You leaned in. You wanted to understand how things worked. But somewhere along the way, the curiosity dried up. Now you coast through your day on autopilot — competent, yes, but not engaged.

Curiosity is your soul's GPS. When it goes quiet, it's not a productivity problem. It's an identity signal.

## Sign 2: Your Values and Your Work Are No Longer Aligned

This one is subtle. You might not even be able to name it at first. But you feel it every Sunday evening — that dull dread that isn't quite anxiety and isn't quite sadness. It's misalignment.

Your values are the things you'd fight for even if no one was watching. When your daily work contradicts those values — even slightly — the cost accumulates. Not in your bank account. In your sense of self.

## Sign 3: You're Asking "Is This It?"

This is the most important sign of all. Not because it means things are hopeless — but because it means your inner compass is still working. The question "Is this it?" is your deepest self refusing to settle.

The answer, by the way, is no. This is not it.

## What Comes Next

Readiness isn't about having a plan. It's about being willing to tell the truth — to yourself, first. If these three signs resonate, the next step isn't a strategy session. It's a conversation.
